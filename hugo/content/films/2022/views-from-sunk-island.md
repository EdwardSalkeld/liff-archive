Michael C Coldwell's photographic exploration of the East Riding coast is a fascinating investigation of a haunted margin between shore and sea, and of obsolescent concrete monuments to war, swept away by the erosion of both memory and land. Can we divine our future in this ruin of our collective amnesia?
